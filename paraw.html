Paraw Regatta Festival: An Attractive Competition of the Boats

Paraw Regatta Festival or Iloilo Paraw Regatta Festival is an annual festival held in February in the Villa de Arevalo district, Iloilo City, Philippines. Its main event is a sailboat race in Iloilo Strait that features the Paraw, a Visayan double outrigger sail boat. It is the oldest traditional craft event in Asia and the largest sailing event in the Philippines.


It is one of Iloilo City's tourism events along with the Paraw Regatta Festival, Kasadyahan Festival, Chinese New Year festival and La Candelaria Fiesta. The Iloilo Paraw Regatta began as a half-day sailboat race but is now a multi-day, multi-event festival.

Paraw Regatta Festival History

The Paraw regatta is a race among seafarers on colorful sailboats called Paraws in the straits between Guimaras Island and the city of Iloilo. The present-day paraw managed to maintain its original design from the sailboats of the first settlers from Borneo who were in search of a peaceful home in 1212 A.D. Surviving the centuries, the paraws have become a vital part of the Filipino seafaring life.

The first race started in 1973 with the mission to preserve the historic value of the paraws. It is held every 3rd weekend of February at Tatoy’s Manokan, Sto. Nino Sur, Villa, Iloilo City. Today, the event has grown from being a boat race to a festival with various interesting activities.

The Iloilo Paraw Regatta Foundation spent tireless hours organizing the 40th edition of the festival. The sailing competition and the slew of side events were not just organized simply to entertain and excite. They were incorporated to the festival to show slices of Ilonggo culture and our love of life and adventure that the rest of the world could appreciate.


Since the dawn of marine travel, the sea has been a constant cradle of inspiration for humanity. The sea provides not only an almost inexhaustible source of food but offers immeasurable sailing adventures. For many, sailing meant freedom, and this is why sailing continues to be a persistent passion for mankind, because of its empowering, exhilarating, challenging and relaxing effects.

Paraw Regatta Festival Guide

There is no room for dead air and boredom in Paraw Regatta Festival when the thunderous drum beats, heart-pounding chants, and world-class Paraw Regatta Festival dance performances begin. And when you’re in Iloilo for the festival, keep these tips in mind to make the most out of your festival experience!

1. Research

Don’t just book that flight and travel to Iloilo empty-handed. Find out the most Instagrammable spots, where to eat, and the best places to stay in Iloilo. Read also some things about Paraw Regatta. Trust us, you’ll enjoy the festivity more if you know and absorb the true Paraw Regatta Festival meaning.

2. Bring water

Don’t let yourself dehydrated. Always bring a water bottle with you because for sure, you will get tired from the endless but convivial walks, dances, and parties throughout the day!

Wear appropriate clothes

Comfort is key to a more fun festival experience! Wear clothes that are light and comfortable enough for you to move freely. Also, if you don’t want to have calluses at the end of the day, avoid wearing heels. Choose a comfortable but tough pair of shoes to keep your feet clean and safe.

4. Wear sunscreen

Unless you want to get a tan, protect your skin from harmful UV rays of the sun by putting on sunblock.

5. Book your accommodations in Iloilo early

Paraw Regatta Festival is one of the country’s biggest festivals so expect that many tourists around the world will visit Iloilo during this time. We suggest that you book your accommodations in Iloilo as early as today.
